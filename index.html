About

I spend a lot of time pondering what I should really be doing. Over the years, I've found a lot of interesting things—nature, animals, science, psychology, and even just the idea of helping people and making a positive impact. Time passed, and I was already a 3rd-year computer science student. There were so many fascinating things to study in tech, and the best path forward was still unclear. But two things were certain: I enjoyed learning and building. In the first years of college, I spent much of my free time programming in Python, creating simple scripts and GUIs that made small tasks more efficient.


Eventually, I realized I didn't need a perfect answer—I just needed to choose something, anything, that complemented my interests and values. I chose cybersecurity. The increasing frequency of ransomware attacks, particularly those targeting hospitals and healthcare institutions, deeply disturbed me. The idea that malicious actors would endanger lives for profit felt like a call to action. Coupled with the news of the growing need for cybersecurity professionals, I found my purpose in protecting others in the digital space.


A strong ethical compass, integrity, and commitment to continous learning guide me on the journey. Cybersecurity is my way to contribute to a safer digital world.
